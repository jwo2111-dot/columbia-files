Urban counties generally have higher incomes and longer lifespans than rural counties in the same state.

Life expectancy

County Health Rankings & Roadmaps 2024 (Robert Wood Johnson Foundation / UW Population Health Institute). 2021–2023 pooled mortality data.

Median household income

US Census Bureau American Community Survey 2022 5-Year Estimates, as compiled by County Health Rankings & Roadmaps 2024.

Urban/rural classification

NCHS Urban–Rural Classification Scheme for Counties (2013). Urban = codes 1–2 (large central & fringe metro). Rural = codes 5–6 (micropolitan & noncore).

County selection

One representative urban county (primary metro county) and one rural county selected per state. Rhode Island and a few other states have limited rural noncore counties; nearest qualifying county used.

Life expectancy values are period life expectancy at birth.  ·  "Urban" counties are the primary county of the state's largest metropolitan area.  ·  "Rural" counties are noncore or micropolitan (no city >50,000).  ·  Hover tooltip shows the income gap and lifespan gap between each state's urban and rural county.
